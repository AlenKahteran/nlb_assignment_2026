When you visit our Websites, small files called cookies are stored on your device. We can read this information during your next visit. We use the information we obtain to personalise the content and functionality of our Websites and to improve the user experience. The information collected may be used for marketing and promotional services and communications (for example, to optimise and improve the user experience as well as provide more attractive offers and services).

Although we do not collect personally identifiable information such as name, address or telephone number through cookies, the information collected through cookies and other tracking technologies is also subject to the provisions of the Personal Data Protection Terms and Conditions.

>Cookie Settings

Cookies are small files usually containing a sequence of letters and numbers that are placed on a user’s browser or device when they visit a website. The main purpose of a cookie is to allow the web server to present personalised web pages to the user, making the experience of visiting the website more personal and responsive to the user’s individual needs. Cookies are also used to help the website operator learn more about how users use its websites and to improve the user’s experience when visiting.

The Website uses its own cookies (sent to the user’s device by a computer or domain directly managed by the Operator), which may be persistent or temporary. Temporary or session cookies last only for the duration of the session and are deleted when the user closes the web browser. Persistent cookies remain in the cookie file of the user’s browser for a longer period of time until they are deleted. This period depends on the user’s choice in the settings of the user’s web browser. Persistent cookies allow information to be transferred to a web server each time someone visits a website. Persistent cookies are also known as tracking cookies.

In addition to our own cookies, GEN-I Websites also use third-party cookies. These cookies are mainly used to analyse website traffic and for marketing purposes. These are cookies that are sent to the user’s device from a computer or domain that is not managed by the Operator, but by another organisation that processes the data obtained from the cookies.

We also share information about your online behaviour with advertising and social networks for the purpose of providing personalised offers and tailored advertising within the advertising and social networks on other websites outside of our domain. However, we do not share your personally identifiable information with these partners. A list of the partners with whom we share such information is set out in the Personal Data Protection Terms and Conditions in Section 7.

This Website uses cookies in a way that allows the user to manage their preferences, using the following groups of cookies according to their purpose:

  • Strictly necessary cookies – These cookies are essential for the full functioning of the Website. Without them, the functionality of the Website would be impaired and it would be impossible to use the portal and its basic functionalities (e.g. logging in). We do not need your consent to place these cookies on your devices as they do not contain personal data and cannot be turned off. The law allows cookies to be placed on devices without consent when it is necessary solely for the purpose of transmitting a message over an electronic communications network or when it is strictly necessary for the provision of an information society service.
  • Marketing cookies - These cookies are set and placed by our advertising partners and other partner companies and are used for the purpose of advanced advertising, for example to display more relevant content to the user on other websites (e.g. Facebook, Google). If you do not consent to the use of these cookies, you will see ads online that are not tailored to your interests.
  • Analytical cookies – Sometimes called statistical research cookies. These cookies are necessary to improve the user experience. They help us collect anonymous information about how users use our Websites. We use them to improve the functionality of our Websites (e.g. to improve the accessibility of the most visited Websites). We need your consent to place these cookies on your devices.
