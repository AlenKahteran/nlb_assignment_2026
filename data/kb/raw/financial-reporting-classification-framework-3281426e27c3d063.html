Open source · FRCF · v3.0 · May 2026

The global standard for classifying corporate disclosures.

An open-source framework covering 11 disclosure types, 31 report types, and 100+ publication types — designed to make the filings of publicly listed companies consistent, comparable, and machine-readable.

Download methodology (PDF, v3.0)
01 / The problem

From fragmentation to a unified standard.

Reporting now spans financial statements, governance, ESG, debt, and market-disclosure rules across dozens of regulators. Existing schemes were built for one slice of that picture.

A fragmented landscape

Categories don't travel.

Regulator-driven taxonomies fragment by jurisdiction. Format-driven taxonomies miss what a report is actually for. Once ESG, governance, and risk disclosures enter the mix, the gaps stop being academic.

A function-oriented framework

Classify by purpose, not by form.

FRCF organises every disclosure into broad functional categories first, then refines into report and publication types. Reports stay regulator-compliant while becoming comparable across markets.

02 / Principles

Four design choices behind the standard.

Built so a single record can be classified consistently — by a human, by a regulator, or by a model — regardless of where it was filed.

2.1 / Hierarchy

Three tiers, one path.

Disclosure type → report type → publication type. 11 → 31 → 100+. Every document resolves to a single deterministic path through the tree.

2.2 / Coverage

Every kind of filing.

Annual reports, ESEF, SEC filings, AGM materials, ESG, debt notices, M&A, regulatory news. The full disclosure surface, not just financial statements.

2.3 / Cross-market

Reads across jurisdictions.

Includes formats specific to ESEF, SEC, and global market-disclosure regimes — without forcing a single regulator's vocabulary on the rest.

2.4 / Comparability

Standardised, by design.

An "Annual Report" in Frankfurt, Stockholm, and New York all resolve to the same FRCF node. Tracking, comparison, and time-series work with no manual mapping layer.

# Load the hierarchical model

# Predict using text + document length

06 / What changed

v3.0, in one table.

v2.0 (December 2025) consolidated eight publication types into broader categories where the underlying disclosure was functionally identical. v3.0 (May 2026) brings the framework into alignment with the production database: it adds a new MA report type for formal merger-compliance filings, redirects two retired codes, and tightens several publication labels. The active taxonomy contains 31 report types.

Previously In v3.0 Rationale
Interim Report; Quarterly Report (10-Q) IR Interim / Quarterly Report Same period-end financials; terminology was jurisdictional.
Annual / Quarterly Financial Statement (10-K-AFS, 10-Q-AFS) RNS Regulatory Filings Stand-alone statements almost always accompanied the parent annual/interim filing; insufficient period information to split.
Business and Financial Review (BFR); Management Discussion & Analysis MDA Management Reports Synonymous narrative management reporting under regional names.
Pre-AGM; Post-AGM (EGM) AGM-R AGM Information Both contain resolutions, voting, and shareholder action; the timing distinction was non-material.
Regulatory News Service; Registration Form (RF); Prospectus (PRS); Foreign Filer Report (FFR) RNS Regulatory Filings Consolidated as the core compliance disclosure channel.
Related Party Transaction (RPT) RNS Regulatory Filings RPT was a downgrade in specificity that lands in the generic regulatory bucket. TAR remains for the active market-facing M&A announcement stream; a new MA covers formal merger-compliance filings.
New in v3.0 MA Merger & Acquisition Formal merger-compliance and tender-offer filings — distinct from TAR's market-facing announcement stream.

Appendix A1 of the methodology PDF documents every change with full rationale.
