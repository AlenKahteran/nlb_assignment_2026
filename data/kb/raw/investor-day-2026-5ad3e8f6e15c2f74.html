NLB Investor Day

7 May 2026, Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Our story behind the numbers

Set against the backdrop of the iconic National Theatre in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina, the third NLB Investor Day took place on Thursday, May 7, 2026, building on a journey that began in Belgrade in 2022 and continued in Ljubljana two years later. More than one hundred global investors, financial analysts, banking experts and key stakeholders from the world’s leading financial centres gathered in Sarajevo, while a couple of hundred followed the conference online – to learn of NLB’s story behind the numbers. This year’s NLB Investor Day focused on the transformation of the NLB Group and the progress made in delivering our 2030 strategy, published two years ago. The discussions highlighted how strategic priorities translate into concrete results, sustainable growth, and long‑term value creation.

About the NLB Investor Day

NLB Investor Day is one of the largest events for financial investors and analysts in the South-eastern Europe and takes place every two years. NLB organised the first such meeting in May 2022 in Belgrade and the second in May 2024 in Ljubljana. On both occasions, more than 200 investors, financial experts and analysts from most of the world’s major financial centres attended, alongside representatives of key regional companies that form the backbone of the local economy. In addition, several hundred participants followed both events online.

 

This year, Sarajevo was chosen as the host city not only for its symbolic role at the crossroads of east and west, cultures and ideas, and, above all, its resilience, but also because it reflects the spirit of the region we at the NLB Group call our home – dynamic, connected, and full of untapped potential. By bringing the event to Sarajevo, NLB Group brings our home region closer to global investors and contributes to the development of strong and resilient local capital markets.

 

Bosnia and Herzegovina has historically been a crossroads of cultures and trade routes. This has not changed; today, however, it also stands at the intersection of capital flows, strategic interests and development opportunities. In the future, its economic potential and connecting role will undoubtedly make a decisive contribution to regional development and competitiveness, as well as to broader European integration, which makes it all the more important that it receives the attention and opportunities it deserves.
